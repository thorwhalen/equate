equate.compare.numeric_geo

Numeric, date, and geospatial comparators (stage ②): turn a domain distance into a [0, 1] similarity via a decay function. Pure standard library.

equate.compare.numeric_geo.exp_decay(scale=1.0, *, distance=<function _default_distance>)[source]

Comparator mapping (non-negative) distance d to exp(-d / scale) in (0, 1].

equate.compare.numeric_geo.gaussian_decay(scale=1.0, *, distance=<function _default_distance>)[source]

Comparator mapping distance d to exp(-(d / scale)^2 / 2) in (0, 1].

equate.compare.numeric_geo.geo_similarity(scale_km=10.0)[source]

Geospatial similarity comparator: exponential decay of haversine distance (km).

equate.compare.numeric_geo.haversine(a, b)[source]

Great-circle distance in km between two (lat, lon) points (degrees).

>>> round(haversine((0.0, 0.0), (0.0, 1.0)), 1)   # 1 deg lon at the equator
111.2
equate.compare.numeric_geo.linear_decay(max_distance=1.0, *, distance=<function _default_distance>)[source]

Comparator mapping distance d to 1 - d / max_distance, clamped to [0, 1].
